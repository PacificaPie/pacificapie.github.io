The bridge between any two of these wasn't planned. 这些之间的桥从来不是规划出来的。

Ruoyang (Pacifica) Zhang  ·  New Haven, CT 张若阳  ·  现居 New Haven

I'm Ruoyang — most people in the U.S. call me Pacifica. I'm a Master's student in Biostatistics & Data Science at Yale, aiming at strategy product roles after graduation.

The shape of my résumé reads strange at first. Applied economics undergrad in Beijing. Risk-strategy intern at Tencent's WeChat. KYC and prospect-modeling intern at an Austrian bank. Research assistant on protein structure data at the Gerstein Lab. Co-author on a multimodal-reasoning paper with two CS teammates at Yale. The bridge between any two of these wasn't planned — it was built one tool at a time as the problem demanded.

A line I keep returning to is from Zhang Zhihe, a Tang-dynasty poet (8th century) who wrote a small cycle of poems titled The Fisherman:

能纵棹，惯乘流，长江白浪不曾忧。

Roughly: "Row hard when there's an oar; drift when there's a current. No fear of the rapids." Zhang called himself the wandering angler in mist and waves. Two postures, equally valid. The first time I read this line I only caught the last clause — something about being unbothered. Years later I reread it and saw the whole thing was about facing the moment as it actually arrives: row when there's something to push against, drift when there isn't, but stay in motion either way.

I think that's how I work. When the problem was a vision-language model I learned LoRA, GRPO, and how to debug a reward function that had silently collapsed to zero variance. When the problem was garbled Chinese in AI-generated images I restructured the generation pipeline instead of fighting the prompt. When the problem was my own job search I built an agent pipeline that now runs every morning without me. When the problem was a B-side merchant tagging system I sat with data scientists and wrote the labeling spec myself. None of these were on a roadmap. They were what was in front of me.

I'm drawn to AI-native product strategy because it sits exactly on the seam between what should be true (business hypothesis), what is true (data, causal inference), and what we can build (engineering). A good product strategist is the person who can hold all three at once and not let any of them rule the others. I'd like to be that person.

A few unsorted things I think are true:

  • Strategy is more honest when you've built the thing once.
  • The best evaluation framework comes from someone who has actually been wrong, in public, before.
  • If I can't run the SQL myself, I'll get bullied by the dashboard.
  • Most "AI" problems are translation problems in disguise.
  • It's possible to be genuinely curious and a real planner. The trick is letting one feed the other.

Outside of work I keep a long-form journal, currently in five-and-counting yearly installments. I row, ski cross-country (recently and badly), and I have opinions about Mexican and Sichuan food that I'm happy to defend.

If you're hiring for strategy product — pricing, evaluation systems, mechanism design, AI-native product — I'd love to talk. ruoyang.zhang@yale.edu.

我叫张若阳，英文名 Pacifica。在耶鲁读生物统计学硕士，毕业目标是策略产品方向。

我的履历乍看有点跳。北京中央财经大学读应用经济学本科， 腾讯微信的违规交易策略实习，奥地利奥合银行的客户挖掘策略实习， Gerstein Lab 的科研助理，最近又和两位耶鲁 CS 同学合作了一篇多模态推理的论文。 这些之间的桥从来不是规划出来的 —— 只是 "问题需要什么就学什么"，一节一节搭出来。

唐代张志和写过一组《渔父》，其中一句我一直记得：

能纵棹，惯乘流，长江白浪不曾忧。

张志和自号"烟波钓徒"。第一次读我只看到最后一句，以为只是 "豁达"。 后来通读才发现这一句其实是说两种姿态：有桨就划，有流就顺，但都不慌。 要么奋起，要么顺势，关键是你一直在动。

我做事的方式大概就是这样的。 问题是视觉语言模型的时候，我去学 LoRA、GRPO、然后在 reward 方差悄悄塌缩成 0 的时候 自己 debug；问题是 AI 生图中文乱码的时候，我重构了生成链路而不是死磕 prompt； 问题是自己找工作的时候，我搭了一条每天早上自己运行的 agent 管线； 问题是 B 端商家标签系统的时候，我自己和数据科学团队一起写标注规范。 这些事情都不在 roadmap 上 —— 它们只是当时在我面前的事情。

AI-native 策略产品吸引我，是因为它正好坐在三件事的交界处： 什么应该是真的（业务假设）、什么实际上是真的（数据与评测）， 以及 我们能造出什么（工程实现）。 一个好的策略 PM 是能同时握住这三股线，不让任何一股偏离主导的人。我希望自己往这个方向走。

一些零散但我认为成立的东西：

  • 亲手做完一遍再谈策略，会更诚实一些。
  • 最好的评估体系，往往是从公开错过的人那里来的。
  • SQL 自己不会跑，就会被 dashboard 教做人。
  • 大部分 "AI 问题" 其实是翻译问题。
  • 真的好奇，又真的能做规划，不矛盾。让其中一个喂养另一个就行。

工作之外我有一本写了五年还在写的年终总结。 会划船，最近开始学 cross-country 滑雪（很烂），对墨西哥菜和川菜有自己愿意为之辩护的偏见。
