GeoQA — When Strong SFT Kills the GRPO Gradient GeoQA —— 强 SFT 之后 GRPO 为什么不动了

The problem

Geometry word problems with diagrams are a hard test for vision-language models. A solver has to read the question, look at the figure, identify which marks matter (right-angle ticks, parallel arrows, congruence indicators), and chain a sequence of theorem-based deductions to a single multiple-choice answer. Traditional OCR-then-LLM pipelines lose most of the visual grammar; even strong general-purpose VLMs underperform.

We worked on the GeoQA benchmark — 3,509 training and 755 test Chinese middle-school geometry problems, each with a diagram and four options. Our backbone was InternVL3-8B. The question was whether a two-stage SFT + RL recipe could give us meaningful gains, on a single A100.

Stage 1 — two-phase SFT

We fine-tuned with LoRA in two phases: a 200-step visual warmup with the language backbone frozen (so the projector and ViT could stabilize without being dragged around by the LLM's evolving preferences), followed by 3 epochs of joint LoRA SFT on the LLM together with ViT and projector.

That alone got us from 73.4% → 79.3% — a typical SFT gain. The interesting story is what happened next.

Stage 2 — and the failure that named itself

We layered GRPO on top with an outcome-based reward (format + answer correctness, weighted 1:2) and a KL term against the SFT reference. The first run did exactly nothing.

Mean training reward at step 0 was already ≈ 3.0 — the maximum attainable. The SFT policy was already answering the majority of training prompts correctly and in-format on every one of the K = 4 rollouts. Within each group, all four rollouts earned the same reward. Within-group standard deviation went to zero. Group-relative advantage went to zero. The policy gradient identically vanished.

Reward saturation: a property of the joint distribution of policy and data that arises whenever a strong imitation prior is followed by outcome-based RL on the same prompts.

We named it. It's not specific to geometry, or to multimodal inputs. The better the SFT, the more tightly the group-relative advantage concentrates around zero. The diagnostic axis isn't the magnitude of the reward but its variance.

Pass@K, repurposed

Pass@K is a metric from code generation: out of K samples, did at least one solve the problem? We took it and used it for something else entirely: a one-shot, training-time data-selection rule.

For every prompt in the GeoQA training set, we drew K = 8 stochastic rollouts from the SFT policy and computed the empirical pass rate p̂(v, q). Three buckets fell out cleanly:

  • Always correct (p̂ = 1): 2,195 prompts (62.6%). These contribute no gradient — every rollout earns max reward.
  • Always wrong (p̂ = 0): 139 prompts (4.0%). Almost no rollout group will contain a correct sample; signal is noisy.
  • Uncertain (0 < p̂ < 1): 1,175 prompts (33.5%). Every group is expected to contain a mix — variance is restored by construction.

We retained only the uncertain bucket. Mean reward at step 0 dropped from 3.0 to 1.75 — confirming that variance was actually back. GRPO on the filtered subset reached 80.2%, +0.9 over SFT and +6.8 over zero-shot.

The diagnostic axis isn't the magnitude of the reward — it's its variance.

What I owned

On a three-person team, I owned the data pipeline (loading and chunking GeoQA-cot, tokenization with the InternVL chat template), the reward design (format + answer extraction with a Chinese-text fallback for malformed tags, the 1:2 weighting), and the Pass@K filtering experiments themselves — running the K = 8 sweep, building the three buckets, and validating that the filtered training set actually unblocked GRPO. I co-authored the technical report.

Why I think this matters for product strategy

The interesting transferable lesson isn't “Pass@K filtering improves RL.” It's that an evaluation system can pass every check and still be silently broken if you don't think about variance, not just magnitude. A reward signal with zero variance is, mathematically, no signal at all. A KPI that everyone is already maxing out is, behaviorally, no KPI at all.

A lot of strategy product work — pricing experiments, ranking algorithm A/Bs, recommendation-engine evaluations — has the same shape. If you only watch the headline metric, you can be staring at “saturation” and not know it. Watching the dispersion is the work.

问题

带图的几何题对视觉语言模型来说是个 stress test。模型需要读题、看图、识别哪些标记是关键的 （直角符号、平行箭头、全等记号），然后串起一连串基于几何定理的推导，得到一个选项。 传统 OCR + LLM 链路丢掉了大部分视觉语法；即便是强力通用 VLM，在这类题上也明显低于平均。

我们在 GeoQA 基准上做实验 —— 3,509 道训练题、755 道测试题的中文初中几何选择题，每题配一张图。 基座是 InternVL3-8B。研究问题是：在单卡 A100 上，两阶段 SFT + RL 这套配方能不能拿到有意义的提升。

第一阶段 —— 两阶段 SFT

我们用 LoRA 分两阶段微调：先做 200 步的视觉 warmup（冻住 LLM，让 projector 和 ViT 能在不被 LLM 变化"拽走"的情况下稳定下来），再做 3 个 epoch 的联合 SFT，LLM 加上 LoRA 一起训练。

单做这一步就把准确率从 73.4% 推到 79.3% —— SFT 的常规收益。真正有意思的是后面发生的事。

第二阶段 —— 一种自己起了名字的失败

我们在 SFT 上叠加 GRPO，用 outcome-based reward（格式 + 答对，1:2 加权），加 KL 正则到 SFT 参考。 第一次跑完，啥也没动。

训练第 0 步的平均 reward 已经是 ≈ 3.0 —— 上限。 SFT 策略已经在大多数训练 prompt 上、4 次 rollout 都答对且格式合规。 组内 4 次 rollout 拿到一样的 reward，组内标准差为 0， group-relative advantage 全部归零，policy gradient 恒为 0。

Reward Saturation：这是 policy 与 data 联合分布的一种性质， 只要"强模仿先验 + 同一批 prompt 上的 outcome-based RL"这个组合出现，它就会出现。

我们给它起了名字。这件事不只属于几何题，也不只属于多模态。 SFT 越强，组内 advantage 越紧地堆在 0 附近。 诊断这件事的轴不是 reward 的大小，是 reward 的方差。

把 Pass@K 借过来当筛选器

Pass@K 本来是代码生成评估指标：K 次采样里至少有一次答对就算通过。 我们把它拿过来做了完全不同的事 —— 一次性的、训练时的数据筛选规则。

对训练集每个 prompt，从 SFT 策略采 K = 8 次，计算经验通过率 p̂(v, q)， 数据自然分成三桶：

  • 始终答对（p̂ = 1）：2,195 题 (62.6%)。 这部分 prompt 不再贡献梯度 —— 每次 rollout 都拿到上限 reward。
  • 始终答错（p̂ = 0）：139 题 (4.0%)。 一组 4 次 rollout 几乎不可能出现至少一次答对，信号噪声大。
  • 不确定（0 < p̂ < 1）：1,175 题 (33.5%)。 每组都期望出现 mix，方差天然非零。

我们只保留"不确定"那一桶。GRPO 第 0 步的平均 reward 从 3.0 掉到 1.75 —— 方差确实回来了。在筛选后的训练集上跑 GRPO，准确率到 80.2%， 相对 SFT +0.9，相对 zero-shot +6.8。

诊断 reward 健不健康，不要看大小，要看方差。

我具体做了什么

三人团队里，我负责数据 pipeline（GeoQA-cot 的加载和切分、按 InternVL chat template 做 tokenization）、 reward 设计（格式判断 + 答案提取 + 中文文本 fallback 应对 tag 出错、1:2 加权）， 以及 Pass@K 筛选的实验本身（K = 8 的扫描、三桶划分、以及验证筛后训练集是真的能让 GRPO 重新动起来）。 论文部分我也参与了撰写。

这件事和策略产品有什么关系

这个项目里我觉得最值得带走的不是"Pass@K 筛选能改善 RL"这个具体结论， 而是这件事：一个评估体系可以指标全绿，但在你不去看方差只看大小的时候， 它已经悄悄失效了。一个方差为 0 的 reward 信号，数学上等于没信号； 一个所有人都已经打满的 KPI，行为上等于没 KPI。

策略产品里很多事情结构都一模一样 —— 定价实验、排序算法 A/B、推荐系统的评估。 如果你只盯着 headline metric，你可能正在盯着 saturation 而不自知。 看分布、看方差，本身才是工作。
